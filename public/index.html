A respectful discussion

Tue 3 Oct 2017, 11:54 AM AEDT

Despite the prime minister's plea to have civilised debate on same-sex marriage, Australia hasn't covered itself in glory over the past X weeks.

It hasn't all been bad - there are many stories of communities coming together to support their LGBTI members.

But the drawn-out postal survey has also brought homophobia into the public sphere in a way we haven't seen before on a national scale.

"There are arguments against [having a plebiscite] but the weakest argument of all, which I think has no basis, is that the Australian people are not capable of having a respectful discussion on this issue ... Australians are able and have demonstrated that they can have a respectful discussion. I am committed to that."

Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbull, August 8, 2017

We've put together the most high-profile evidence that not everyone shared the PM's commitment to respect.
